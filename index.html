  1. Early Life: Born on May 25, 1899, in Churulia, Bengal Presidency (now in West Bengal, India), Nazrul faced poverty early in life. Despite these hardships, he developed a passion for literature and music.

  2. Literary Contributions: Nazrul's works include poetry, songs, novels, and plays. He is renowned for his fiery and revolutionary poems that inspired the struggle for independence against British colonial rule. His notable works include the poem "Bidrohi" (The Rebel) and his anthology "Agnibeena" (The Fiery Lute).

  3. Musical Influence: Nazrul was also a prolific composer and musician, contributing significantly to Bengali music. He created a new genre called "Nazrul Geeti" or "Nazrul Sangeet," which includes songs on diverse themes such as devotion, love, and rebellion.

  4. Political Activism: Nazrul was actively involved in India's independence movement. He joined the Indian National Army and used his writings to inspire people to rise against oppression and injustice.

  5. Personal Struggles: Despite his popularity, Nazrul faced numerous personal challenges. He was imprisoned for his revolutionary activities, and later in life, he suffered from an unknown illness, possibly Pick's disease, which led to a loss of speech and movement.

  6. Legacy: Kazi Nazrul Islam's legacy is celebrated in both Bangladesh and India. His works continue to inspire people, and his contributions to literature, music, and the fight for justice are widely acknowledged. Bangladesh declared him its national poet, and his birthday is celebrated as "Nazrul Jayanti."

  7. Death: Kazi Nazrul Islam passed away on August 29, 1976, in Dhaka, Bangladesh. He is remembered as a versatile genius whose works transcended cultural and national boundaries.
